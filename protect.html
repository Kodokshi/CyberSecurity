Cybersecurity Protection

Attack Targets: How to Protect Yourself

  • Use strong, unique passwords for each account.
  • Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) for added security.
  • Keep software and operating systems updated.
  • Be cautious when clicking on links or downloading files from unknown sources.
  • Use antivirus software and firewalls to block potential threats.

Phishing Attacks: How to Protect Yourself

  • Never click on suspicious links or attachments.
  • Use spam filters to detect and block phishing emails.
  • Verify the legitimacy of requests by contacting the sender directly through official channels.
  • Keep an eye on website URLs to ensure they are secure (look for “https://” in the address bar).
  • Report phishing attempts to your IT department or service provider.

Malware Infections: How to Protect Yourself

  • Install and maintain reputable antivirus and anti-malware software.
  • Regularly scan your devices for malware.
  • Keep your operating system, browser, and other software updated.
  • Avoid downloading software from untrusted sources.
  • Be cautious with email attachments and links, even from known contacts.
  • Back up your data regularly to an external drive or cloud storage.
